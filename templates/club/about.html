About the Heritage Tomato Club

The Heritage Tomato Club is a small, friendly, UK-focused community dedicated to preserving flavourful, rare, and historic tomato varieties. We support growers of all levels — whether you have a greenhouse, an allotment, or just a sunny kitchen windowsill.

Our mission

Supermarket tomatoes prioritise transport, yield, and uniformity — but they’ve lost much of the flavour and diversity that once existed in kitchen gardens across the UK and beyond.

Our mission is to keep heritage and heirloom tomatoes in active cultivation by sharing seeds, knowledge, and stories. Each saved seed keeps a piece of living history alive.

As a community-led project, we grow slowly and intentionally — improving the club with the help, feedback, and contributions of our members.

Preserve diversity

We prioritise open-pollinated heritage varieties, many of which are at risk of disappearing from mainstream cultivation.

Share knowledge

Members share sowing tips, grow journals, space-saving techniques, greenhouse advice, and seasonal encouragement.

Keep stories alive

Heritage tomatoes often come with tales — who saved them, where they travelled, and why they were loved. We try to preserve those stories alongside the seeds.

How the club works

Membership gives you access to the Seed Library, seasonal seed offers, and club-only updates. We don’t aim to be a large social network — our focus is on a warm, helpful, small-scale community.

  • Choose a membership tier based on your growing interest
  • Receive heritage varieties suited to UK conditions
  • Grow, save seed, and share your results with the club
  • Help keep rare tomato lines in active circulation

In time, we’ll expand the Seed Library, release growing guides, and support members in saving, drying, and storing seed properly.
